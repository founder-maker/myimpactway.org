Healing Farm & Early Childhood Rescue (Kenya)

Impactway’s Healing Farm is a specialized rescue, healing, and early development center for Kenya’s most vulnerable street children, ages 0–10. We replace fear and instability with trust, routine, and unconditional care.

Impact: Restoring Emotional Security and Stability

The earliest years are critical. Our trauma-informed program is designed for deep psychological healing, giving children the cognitive scaffolding necessary to transition successfully into primary school (a key indicator of long-term success).

  • **Emotional Transformation:** Trauma-informed counseling combined with daily interaction (animal care, gardening) is proven to increase emotional regulation in traumatized children within six months.
  • **Cognitive Readiness:** Phase 3 focuses on early literacy and cooperative play, preparing children for formal education.
  • **Verifiable Outcome (M&E):** We track metrics like **Weight Gain**, **Improved Sleep Quality**, **Reduced Hypervigilance**, and **Attachment to Caregivers** (measured by a registered therapist) to confirm emotional stabilization.

Program Model: The 12-Month Path to Healing

Phase 1: Rescue & Stabilization (Weeks 1–4)

Immediate objectives: **Safety**, **Nourishment**, **Regulation**. Activities include emergency medical care, establishing a predictable daily routine, and sensory play to reduce fear.

Phase 2: Trust & Regulation (Months 2–3)

Focus: **Emotional Stabilization**. Core activities like gardening, music, and daily animal interaction focused on building trust, regulating nervous systems, and fostering secure attachments.

Phase 3: Development & Learning (Months 4–12)

Focus: **Cognitive Growth**. Includes early literacy, art therapy, and social skills development, preparing the child for a successful transition to formal education.

Year 1 Operational Budget (Summary)

Our budget is designed for high accountability and lean operations, ensuring maximum impact per dollar donated. Full financial reporting is available for corporate partners.

  • **Caregiver & Staff Wages:** $45,000 (Covers 1:4 caregiver ratio, trauma therapist, program manager)
  • **Food & Nutritional Support:** $12,000 (Dedicated budget for early childhood nourishment and special dietary needs)
  • **Medical & Emergency Fund:** $6,000 (Covers rescue, initial medical assessments, and emergency transport)
  • **Early Education & Activity Materials:** $3,100 (For therapeutic play, art, and cognitive stimulation)
  • **Facility & Utilities (Rent/Power/Water):** $10,000
  • **Total Estimated Budget (Year 1):** $76,100

Risk Management and Safeguarding

Child safety and program security are our highest priorities. We have a robust risk mitigation strategy including strict staff vetting, a mandatory 1:4 child-to-caregiver ratio, and formal partnerships with local clinics.
