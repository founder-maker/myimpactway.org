Orphan Digital Literacy Program (Lombok, Indonesia)

This program bridges the digital divide for orphaned adolescents (ages 13–17) by providing essential digital skills needed for employment. We utilize a mobile 15-laptop lab.

Impact: Building Economic Independence

Digital literacy is the foundation for economic participation. Our program directly addresses the challenge of youth aging out of care without employable skills.

  • Economic Opportunity: Students gain skills applicable to entry-level office work or remote assistance.
  • Gender Equity Focus: We commit to training **120 girls annually** (66% of total students).
  • Verifiable Outcome (M&E): We track Skill Achievement scores and Job-Ready Resume completion.

Program Structure: High-Impact and Scalable

Annual Impact Stats:

Students Trained Annually: 180 (4 cycles of 45 students each)

Cost Per Student: Approximately $42 USD
