Orphan Digital Literacy Program (Lombok, Indonesia)

This program bridges the digital divide for vulnerable orphaned adolescents (ages 13–17) by providing essential digital skills needed for education, employment, and economic opportunity. We utilize a mobile 15-laptop lab to bring training directly into orphanages.

Impact: Building Economic Independence and Digital Equity

Digital literacy is not a luxury—it is the foundation for economic participation. Our program directly addresses the challenge of youth aging out of care without employable skills, providing a clear pathway to independence and sustainable livelihoods.

  • Economic Opportunity: By focusing on productivity tools and career skills (resume creation, online portfolios), 100% of graduating students gain skills immediately applicable to entry-level office work or remote assistance.
  • Gender Equity Stat: We commit to **training 120 girls annually** (66% of total students), ensuring young women have equal access to high-demand skills and the economic independence technology offers.
  • Verifiable Outcome (M&E): We track **Pre- and Post-Program Skill Achievement** scores and gather data on the percentage of students who successfully complete a **Job-Ready Resume** and an online portfolio.

Program Structure: High-Impact and Scalable

Annual Impact Stats:

Students Trained Annually: 180 (4 cycles of 45 students each)

Instructional Hours Delivered: 10,800 total hours

Cost Per Student: Approximately $42 USD (annual operational cost)

Curriculum Focus: Practical and Job-Ready

  1. **Digital Foundations:** Essential computer and file management mastery.
  2. **Productivity:** Google Docs, Sheets, and professional email etiquette.
  3. **Creative & Career:** Canva basics, digital portfolio, and resume writing.
  4. **Digital Citizenship:** Online safety, privacy, and avoiding scams.
